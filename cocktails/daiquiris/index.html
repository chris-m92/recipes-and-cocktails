    Daiquiri No. 3

    The Bar Book Page 28

    Not yet tried

    History

    At the Floridita bar in Havana, Cuba, in the 1930s and 1940s, the daiquiri was king, and an extensive daiquiri menu was developed there, due in large part to bartenders like Constantino Ribalaigua Vert. His famed Daiquiri No. 3 was consumed in massive quantities by Ernest Hemingway, though he took his "without sugar and double the rum," which became the legendary Hemingway, or Papa doble, daiquiri.

    Glass/Ice

    • Old Fashioned Glass
    • Crushed Ice

    Ingredients

    • 2 oz White Rum
    • 1/2 oz 2:1 Simple Syrup
    • 1/2 oz Lime Juice
    • 1/4 oz Grapefruit Juice
    • 1 tsp Luxardo Maraschino Liqueur
    • 1 Lime Wedge

    Instructions

    1. Combine the ingredients in a cocktail shaker
    2. Shake with ice cubes
    3. Strain over the old-fashioned glass filled with crushed ice
    4. Garnish with the lime wedge
    5. Serve with a straw

    La Bomba Daiquiri

    Death & Co. Page 262

    Not yet tried

    Glass/Ice

    • Coupe Glass
    • No Ice

    Ingredients

    • 5 Raspberries
    • 2 oz Barbancourt White Rum
    • 3/4 oz Lime Juice
    • 1/2 oz Simple Syrup
    • 1 teaspoon Pomegranate Molasses
    • 1 Lime Wheel and Raspberry Flag

    Instructions

    1. In a shaker, gently muddle the raspberries
    2. Add the remaining ingredients and shake with ice
    3. Double strain into a coupe
    4. Garnish with the lime wheel and raspberry flag
