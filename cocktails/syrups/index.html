    Acacia Honey Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 276

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups Acacia Honey
    • 1 cup Warm Water

    Instructions

    1. In a bottle or other container with a tight lid, combine the honey and water
    2. Shake vigorously until the honey is dissolved

    Banana Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 276

    Ingredients

    • 1 Banana, sliced
    • 2 cups Cane Sugar
    • 2 cups Water

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, combine banana, cane sugar, and water
    2. Bring to a simmer, then remove from heat, cover, and let cool to room temperature
    3. Refrigerate overnight
    4. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve

    Blueberry Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 276

    Ingredients

    • 5 cups Blueberries
    • 1 cup Sugar
    • 1 cup Water
    • 2 tbsp Apple Cider Vinegar

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan combine the blueberries, sugar, and water
    2. Crush the berries with a potato masher
    3. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 15 minutes
    4. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve, pressing the berries to extract as much liquid as possible
    5. Return the strained mixture to the pan and simmer for 10 minutes
    6. Stir in the apple cider vinegar and simmer for 2 minutes

    Braeburn Apple Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 276

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups Water
    • 2 cups Sugar
    • 5 Braebrun Apple Peels and Cores

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, combine the water and sugar, bring to a simmer
    2. Stir in the peels and cores from the apples and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 30 minutes
    3. Let cool to room temperature
    4. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve

    Cane Sugar Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 276

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups Organic Cane Sugar (often labeled "evaporated cane juice"; this is different from turbinado sugar)
    • 1 cup Water

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, combine the sugar and water
    2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly and without bringing to a boil, until the sugar is dissolved

    Cinnamon Bark Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 276

    Ingredients

    • 1 oz Cassia Cinnamon Sticks
    • 2 cups Water
    • 2 cups Superfine Sugar

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, muddle the cinnamon sticks until it's broken up into shards
    2. Add the water and superfine sugar
    3. Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally
    4. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer gently for 4 minutes
    5. Remove from the heat and let stand overnight
    6. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve

    Cumin Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 6 tbsp Cumin Seeds
    • 2 cups Sugar
    • 2 cups Water

    Instructions

    1. In a dry skillet, toast the cumin seeds over medium-low heat until fragrant
    2. In a saucepan, combine the sugar and water and bring to a boil, stirring constantly until the sugar is dissolved
    3. Remove from the heat and stir in the cumin seeds
    4. Cover and let stand for 6 hours
    5. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve

    Curried Ginger Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 2 tbsp Tellicherry Black Peppercorns
    • 2 tbsp of Grains of Paradise
    • 2 tbsp Coriander Seeds
    • 1 1/2 cups Demerara Sugar
    • 1/2 cup Superfine Sugar
    • 2 cups Hot Water
    • 4 Sprigs of Fresh Curry Leaves, muddled

    Instructions

    1. In a suacepan, toast the peppercorns, grains of paradise, and coriander seeds over medium heat until fragrant, about 2 to 3 minutes
    2. Add the demerara sugar, superfine sugar, and hot water
    3. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly, then lower the heat and simmer for 2 minutes
    4. Remove from the heat and add the fresh curry leaves to the pot
    5. Cover and let stand for 1 hour
    6. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve, pressing firmly on the solids to extract as much flavor as possible

    Espresso-Infused Demerara Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 4 oz Espresso-Roast Coffee Beans, coarsly ground
    • 2 cups Room-temperature Water
    • Demerara Syrup, to taste

    Instructions

    1. Transfer the ground coffee beans to a container
    2. Add 2 cups of water and stir well
    3. Cover and let stand for 12 to 16 hours
    4. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer
    5. Stir in Demerara syrup to taste

    Ginger Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 1/2 cup Fresh Ginger Juice (Requires about 24 oz ginger root)
    • 1 cup Superfine Sugar

    Instructions

    1. Make the ginger juice either by using an extractor or finely grating and squeezing the juice
    2. Put the juice in a blender
    3. Add 1 cup of superfine sugar
    4. Blend until the sugar is dissolved

    Grenadine

    Death & Co.

    Death & Co. Page 284

    Ingredients

    • 4 cups Organic Unfiltered, Unsweetened Pomegranate Juice
    • 3 cups Organic Cane Sugar
    • 6 oz Pomegranate Molasses
    • 8 Orange Twists (Half-Dollar Sized)

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, combine both ingredients
    2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly and without bringing to a boil, until the sugar is dissolved
    3. Remove from the heat and let cool to room temeperature
    4. Transfer to a container
    5. Stir in the pomegranate molasses
    6. Squeeze in the orange twists and discard
    7. Stir well
    8. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks

    The Bar Book

    The Bar Book Page 100

    Ingredients

    • 16 oz Fresh Pomegranate Juice or POM Wonderful 100% Pomegranate Juice
    • 16 oz Raw Sugar
    • 2 oz Pomegranate Molasses
    • 1 tsp Orange Blossom Water

    Instructions

    1. Heat the pomegranate juice slightly in a small saucepan
    2. Add the sugar, molasses, and orange blossom water, stirring until the sugar is dissolved
    3. Allow to cool and bottle
    4. Store in the refrigerator for up to 1 month

    Gum Syrup

    The Bar Book Page 93

    Ingredients

    • 2 oz Gum Arabic (Gum Acacia)
    • 6 oz Water
    • 12 oz Superfine Sugar

    Instructions

    1. In a small plastic container, combine the gum arabic with 2 oz of water
    2. Stir with a chopstick to blend
    3. Cover and let sit for 48 hours or until the gum arabic is completely dissolved
    4. In a small saucepan, combine the sugar and remining 4 oz water
    5. Heat gently until the sugar begins to dissolve
    6. Fold in the gum arabic mixture
    7. Remove from heat immediately
    8. Let cool
    9. Bottle in a plastic squeeze bottle
    10. Store in the refrigerator indefinitely

    Hibiscus Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups Water
    • 3 1/2 oz Dried Hibiscus Flowers
    • 2 cups Superfine Sugar

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil
    2. Remove from the heat and stir in the hibiscus flowers
    3. Cover and let stand for 20 minutes
    4. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve
    5. Return to the saucepan
    6. Stir in 2 cups superfine sugar and cook over low heat, stirring constantly and without bringing to a boil, until the sugar is dissolved

    Mint Syrup

    The Bar Book Page 96

    Ingredients

    • 12 oz 1:1 Simple Syrup
    • 5 Large or 7 Medium Sprigs Fresh Mint

    Instructions

    1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil
    2. Make an ice bath in a medium bowl
    3. Grasping the stem ends of the mint sprigs, immerse the leafy ends completely in the boiling water for 15 seconds
    4. Remove and immediately submerge in the ice bath for 1 mintue
    5. Remove from the ice bath and pat dry with paper towels
    6. Blend the blanched mint leaves and simple syrup in a blender on high for 1 minute
    7. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth into a plastic squeeze bottle
    8. Refrigerate for up to 1 month

    Orgeat

    Death & Co. Page 278

    History

    Orgeat is an almond-based syrup that has been part of cocktail culture since the mid-nineteenth century. It's a key ingredient in the vintage Japanese Cocktail (brandy, orgeat, and Angostura bitters) and especially the mai tai and its tiki brethren. Made primarily from water and almonds, along with other ingredients such as orange flower water and cognac, it has a unique flavor that's more t han the sum of its ingredients and something altogether individual. It's milky and nutty but not cloying. It deepens the flavors of other ingredients and bridges magically between refreshing and savory, creating an etheral mouthfeel that makes ingredients meld in unexpected ways, and at Death & Co, we use it in many styles of cocktails.

    Ingredients

    • 12 oz Toasted Almond Milk
    • 16 oz Superfine Sugar
    • 2 1/2 tsp Pierre Ferrand Ambre Cognac
    • 2 1/2 tsp Lazzaroni Amaretto
    • 1/4 tsp Rose Water

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, combine the almond milk and sugar
    2. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally and without bringing to a boil, until the sugar is dissolved
    3. Remove from the heat and stir in the cognac, amaretto, and rose water
    4. Store in the refrigerator for up to one month

    The Bar Book Page 59

    Quinine Tincture

    Ingredients

    • 6 grams Powdered Cinchona Bark (Red will be more assertive, yellow is milder and less bitter)
    • 150 ml Vodka

    Instructions

    1. Dissolve the powdered cinchona bark in the vodka, mix well
    2. Strain through a paper coffee filter fitted into a strainer (or filter cone, if you have one) into a large enough container to accommodate the final volume of vodka
    3. This could take up to 1 hour

    Aromatics

    Ingredients

    • 20 grams Citric Acid
    • 10 grams Whole Gentian Root
    • 2 grams Ceylon Soft-Stick Cinnamon, broken by hand into small pieces
    • 30 grams Lemon Peel
    • 30 grams Grapefruit Peel
    • 400 grams Sugar
    • 500 ml Water

    Instructions

    1. Combine all the ingredients in a medium saucepan
    2. Heat over medium heat just until boiling
    3. Reduce the heat to low
    4. Simmer, covered, for 20 minutes
    5. Strain out the solids and let the liquid cool

    Syrup

    Instructions

    1. Stir 1 1/2 oz of the quinine tincture into the cooled aromatics
    2. Bottle and seal tightly
    3. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks

    Scarlet Glow Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups Water
    • 4 heaping tbsp Scarlet Glow Herbal Tea In Pursuit of Tea
    • 2 cups Superfine Sugar

    Instructions

    1. In a saucepan, bring the water to a boil
    2. Remove from the heat and stir in the tea
    3. Cover and let stand for 6 hours
    4. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve
    5. Add the superfine sugar and shake or stir until the sugar is dissovled

    Vanilla Syrup

    Death & Co. Page 277

    Ingredients

    • 1 Tahitian Vanilla Bean
    • 2 cups Water
    • 2 cups Superfine Sugar

    Instructions

    1. Split the vanilla bean in half lengthwise and put it in a saucepan
    2. Add the water and superfine sugar
    3. Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally
    4. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer gently for 4 minutes
    5. Remove from the heat and let stand overnight
    6. Strain through a cheesecloth-lined sieve
